Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, is an esteemed Indian cricketer, renowned globally for his remarkable prowess on the field. As a former captain of the Indian national cricket team, Kohli has left an indelible mark on the sport. His adeptness as a right-handed batsman and occasional medium-fast bowler has earned him widespread acclaim. Presently, he represents Royal Challengers Bengaluru in the IPL and Delhi in domestic cricket. Kohli's illustrious career boasts numerous records, including the highest run-scorer in T20I and IPL. Additionally, he holds the prestigious title for the most centuries in ODI cricket, standing as a beacon of excellence. Notably, Kohli's contributions were pivotal in India's triumphs in the 2011 Cricket World Cup and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy. Furthermore, his leadership led India to secure the ICC Test mace consecutively in 2017, 2018, and 2019, cementing his legacy as one of cricket's finest.

Roots and Rearing

Virat Kohli, a prominent figure in Indian cricket, was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, marking the beginning of a journey that would shape the landscape of cricket in the country.

Kohli's roots lie in a Punjabi Hindu family, reflecting the rich cultural tapestry of his upbringing and familial influences on his life and career.

Prem Nath Kohli, Virat's father, pursued a career as a criminal lawyer, while his mother, Saroj Kohli, dedicated her time to managing the household, creating a nurturing environment for Kohli and his siblings.

Alongside Virat, the Kohli family includes his elder brother Vikas and elder sister Bhawna, constituting a close-knit unit that provided unwavering support throughout his journey.

Uttam Nagar, a vibrant locality in Delhi, served as the backdrop for Kohli's formative years, shaping his early experiences and laying the foundation for his future endeavors.

Even in his early years, Kohli exhibited an exceptional talent for cricket, displaying innate skills and a deep passion for the sport that would define his trajectory in the years to come.

At the tender age of nine, Kohli embarked on his cricketing journey by joining the West Delhi Cricket Academy, a pivotal moment that marked the beginning of his formal training under the mentorship of coach Rajkumar Sharma.

Despite encountering initial challenges and setbacks, Kohli's determination and resilience propelled him forward, underscoring his unwavering commitment to realizing his cricketing aspirations.

The devastating loss of his father, Prem Nath Kohli, to a cerebral attack on December 18, 2006, marked a profound turning point in Kohli's life, shaping his perspective and fueling his drive to excel in cricket in honor of his father's memory.

From Meera Bagh, Paschim Vihar, where the Kohli family resided until 2015, to their subsequent relocation to Gurgaon, each phase of Kohli's journey reflects the unwavering support of his family and the enduring spirit that defines his remarkable career.
